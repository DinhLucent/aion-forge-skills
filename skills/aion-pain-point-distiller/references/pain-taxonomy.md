# Pain Taxonomy

Use this taxonomy to avoid vague problems.

- Operational pain: repeated manual work, handoffs, interrupts, queue buildup.
- Cognitive load: users scan too much, reconcile many states, or memorize rules.
- Safety risk: wrong action, delayed action, missed escalation, unsafe automation.
- Engineering friction: fragile integration, unclear contracts, expensive debugging.
- Workflow delay: waiting, re-entry, approvals, batch timing, missing dependencies.
- Data quality issue: missing, stale, duplicated, ambiguous, or inconsistent data.
- Audit/compliance gap: missing traceability, approvals, role checks, retention.
- Maintenance burden: high configuration cost, brittle customizations, unclear ownership.

Anti-patterns:

- "Users need a dashboard" without the task and blocker.
- "Add AI" without describing workload, risk, or error reduction.
- "Improve UX" without naming the workflow cost.
